Chess

Objective

Checkmate the opponent king, meaning the king is in check and has no legal escape.

Core Rules

  • White moves first, then turns alternate.
  • Each piece has fixed movement patterns.
  • You cannot make a move that leaves your own king in check.

Special Moves

  • Castling: king and rook move together if neither has moved and path is clear.
  • En passant: special pawn capture available immediately after a two-square pawn move.
  • Promotion: pawn reaching back rank promotes, usually to a queen.

Game End Conditions

  • Checkmate: current player loses.
  • Stalemate: draw.
  • Draw by repetition, insufficient material, or move-limit rules depending on implementation.

Strategy Tips

  • Develop minor pieces early and control the center.
  • Castle to protect your king.
  • Blunder-check every move for tactical threats.
